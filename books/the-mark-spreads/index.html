The Mark Spreads

Seer Frights #3 · by B. Carter

Milo Vance is invisible. His own homeroom teacher has called him the wrong name since September, and he’s never corrected her — raising his hand is more attention than he can survive. The only person who really sees him is Reggie, his loud best friend since second grade.

Then, in the dark back row of a dying arcade, a machine nobody remembers lights up just for Milo and prints one tattoo no catalog shows: a black branching mark, and a little slip of paper with six words. Don’t let it touch broken skin.

The tattoo won’t wash off. And the first time Milo wears it where people can see, the perfect comeback finally arrives on time. He stands up to his bully. The whole hall watches. For the first time in his life, Milo is the kid people look at.

He doesn’t care that it won’t come off. That’s the trap. Because the mark is spreading.

Up his arm, past his elbow, toward his throat — every day a little bolder, a little crueler, a little less himself. Reggie sees it before Milo will admit it, and Reggie will do anything to get his friend back. But you can’t rescue a kid who’s finally, for the first time, happy. There’s one mercy in those six words, one lock on one door. And Milo is a single drop of blood from opening it.

A creepy, funny, genuinely unsettling middle-grade horror story about being seen, being eaten, and the friend who wouldn’t stop scrubbing. Every chapter ends on a scare and half of them are real. Reads as a standalone — no prior books required.
